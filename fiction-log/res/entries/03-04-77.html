Red's Logpad. Entry 03-04-77

Back to shallow waters. Maintenance, said the old man. Most of the crew took the opportunity and skidaddled out of here. There was one newbie tho. An assistant. Young lad, and eager. And a bit slow. I give him a week of life at most. At least we have time to rest.

The old man heard the prayers! He added a common sleeping room. He did throw out the old garden though... Well, at least we have a proper bed and and a drawer each. But the whole electrical broke down again. Need to help Miser if we ever want to leave this place.

Fully resupplied and fixed. And refitted. A new sonar and some mystery sonic weapon. Miser's been working in the engine room almost nonstop.
"Junction box, electrical. Broke down again."
"Pump, ballast A, needs fixing again."
"Batteries need some love."
Was all I could hear on the radio.
Heard we will be ferrying some cargo for them miners. At least it'll get us moving.

That was a short trip. And, frankly, boring. Armed myself and sat down in me chair. I love that chair. It's a really comfortable chair. Railgun operator's chair. The beds are on the opposite side of the sub, so I think I still will be sleeping here mostly. Anyway, twas just a few crawlers. One got inside. Maybe I will have time to gut it. Could make a tasty grilled crawler breasts.

Very few people on the sub now. Maybe six? They constantly come and go. Easy to lose track. Sooner or later they will all end up a fish feed or some pirate's shooting target dummy. Little time to write with. When am not shooting need to fix boxes and pumps.

Back at a station again. Studied the map for a bit. A few supposed separatist outposts are nearby. Damned anarchists. We could raid one of them. They are just taking up space, people there are a waste of oxygen.

Another supply run. Not much happened. Just a few spiners and crawlers too slow to harm the ship. But all this maintenance... All I hear on radio chatter is "that's broken, this' broken". Need to find some cheap labour. There always used to be a bunch of younglings willing to sail with us. Where the hell are they now?

Passed through a seppie outpost. "Nay, not yet" said the old man. Got his hands on a good contract. Explosives transport. Judging by contents of the boxes, must be for some terrorist or military group. I think I know what's going to happen. Time to preprare some explosives of my own.

That was a massacre. Cig brought out two military hard suits. Thought I will never see them again. Brings back memories from my military times... He planted explosives on their generator. Miser overloaded their grid with our own. The explosives didn't go off. Two blown off with a grenade. Security peppered with LMG rounds. Shipwrights dealt with a simple rifle. Rest - an axe or LMG. Clowns too. Time to clean up. Lots of supplies to loot here.

That was a hard day of work, gathering all the bodies. We stripped them of everything and dumped into the submarine. Ammo and explosives for the crew. Oxygen tanks for refilling. Rest for melting. Now time to sleep.

Melting all these supplies... At least we are not lacking supplies now. Lurked around the outpost before we left. Clowns were hiding a weird thing there. A cannon. Very old one. Made out of bronze. Read about these. Old Earth tech, from before atom. Needs some repairs but... I wonder how effective it is. Cast some cannon balls, prepped some smokeless powder. It's surprisingly light for a bronze equivalent of at least two rocket launchers in size. Just need to find a good shooting target.

Fuck. Got up onto the outpost, aimed... Boom! Recoil flew me across two rooms. Miser was just passing through. Didn't hit, luckily. Not directly at least. Ripped his little finger off as if it was a crispy raptor rib. He was so surprised he didn't even scream at first. The body I hung up as a target tho... Hit the torso, flew across the room, pierced the wall. The arms stayed in place. Ripped off. Dangerous thing. I like it.

Finished melting, repairing, fixing Miser. We dumped the bodies into the depths below. Time to move on. Counted everything we found up there. All in all - a waste of ammo. At least we did a good deed and rid the region of a few seppies.
