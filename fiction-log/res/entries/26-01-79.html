Red's Logpad. Entry 25-03-77

Who would've thought? Hired meself for checkin' the incoming ships' manifests, just to kill some time. First thing in the morning a ship with designation RE-3 docks, Captain's name Cig. I had to check it thrice, but everything checked out. Old man's back, just in time too. Never really told anyone if I'll come back but... we'll see.

Didn't take any convin' really. Old man was excited to show me the new ship they cobbled together more than anything. Doubt the docking bay staff will miss me... nor will the seccies. Or criminals. Truth be told the whole city's probably glad am gone... and am not gonna drop by to settle my debts anytime soon.

The ship is a marvel. Smaller than RE-2, but nicer. Less empty. Though I do miss my blast doors. It'll take some time to adjust. Everything's misplaced. Even armoury lockers are in different places. The crew must've been bad with money too, there's barely anything here. Mere six rubber shotgun shells? And that marvel also has some quirks to iron out. Whoever did the electrical, fucked up the whole way. Fucking railgun periscope switch controlling gunnery lights? And the boxes keep frying up. At least if I close my doors I can't hear the alarm. Yeah, my doors. We've crew quarters now. And there're some beds in the lounge. Comfy. There's even a gardening space.

Ship's navigation's like a dream. Had to take over for the old man for a bit. Smooth sailing is like saying nothing about it. It's not that awful, unsteerable behemoth RE-2. Feels more like a good ol' Humpback or Dugong. If not for the constant power issues, I'd say am in love again. Well, best I can do is take a nap while they deal with it. Am not walking into that airless deathtrap they call "fire prevention system".

Ouch. Still have to get used to the new ship layout in the dark...

Not much of note happened that first sail... though the radio's fucked I think. Audio feedback's stuck on, keep hearing myself over the headset. There was a wreck but nothing of note in it, just a bit of ethanol. Another port, another break. Shorter one. And first pay. And all of it got blown for fuel rods. It was a good deal though... that shithole didn't even had a merchant, just some shady guy selling station's supplies. Guess 900 marks wasn't a bad price for four. time for a good nap... or two.
